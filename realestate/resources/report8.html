Home Inspections Just Don't Benefit Buyers
 

When an agent is contacted by a buyer, the first thing the agent does is look up properties on the computer that fit the buyers price range. Depending on the market conditions, the agent may look a bit above the buyer’s upper range, but not by much.

Now many sellers will say, “we can always come down” when working with their agent on a price, but a higher price may just be putting you out of the running.

Your agent should prepare a CMA, or comparative market analysis, for you. The CMA will show houses of like size, location and features that have sold in the last six months. This is the most important category because it is also what appraisers will look at during the closing process. The CMA also shows homes that are currently on the market, which is your competition.

Finally, the CMA will show homes that were on the market in this time period but did not sell. Price isn’t always the factor, but this category will show you the upper limit.

Good agents know that the inventory and houses that start too high get a bad reputation. Be realistic in accordance with your goals.
