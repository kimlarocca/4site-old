Home Inspections Just Don't Benefit Buyers
 

While dreaming can be fun, there are times when it is best to be realistic. Certainly shopping for a home and the home loan is one of those times when reality is the best path to take.

How much do you have for a down payment? How good is your credit? How much debt are you already carrying? How big of a payment is realistic for you? These are some of the factors that you can bring with you when you speak with a lender. Having that information can then yield an amount for a loan, which will determine what sort of house you can then search for.

This is where you should start your home purchase. While it might be fun to look at homes above your price range, it’s really no fun to be “house poor.”

You might qualify for a loan for that dream home, but it’s good to remember that there are other expenses. You might want to do some improvements. There are always taxes and insurance. And don’t forget maintenance. There is no landlord to call when you are the owner and repairs are no respecters of your checkbook.

Basically the advice is to try to separate the process from emotion. That is never easy, no matter how many homes you may have purchased. You might not be completely successful, but it can save you the headaches of being overextended.
