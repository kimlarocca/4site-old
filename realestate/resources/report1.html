Home Inspections Just Don't Benefit Buyers
 

Yes, everyone says that buying a home is the biggest investment you’re likely to make in your life. But where do you start?

You’ve saved up some money for your down payment. You’ve cleaned up your credit report. Now what?

Your first step should be finding a lender and getting a pre-approval. This will let you know the price range of your new home. You’ll also know how much you will need for payments, taxes and insurance.

The next step is to establish some of the features that you would like your home to have. Once you have finished the list, go through it again and separate the “must haves” from the “nice to haves.”

Finally, go through the above step, but this time, do it in relation to the location and conveniences of the home. How long of a commute are you willing to tolerate? Is there a school district in which you want to live? Do you need to be close to shopping? Is there a church nearby? Then go through this list and do the nice and must haves.

A final tip regarding commutes - most people view homes on the weekends. Traffic patterns are very different from the weekday. If there is any question about the commute, rent a hotel room on a Sunday. Bring your work clothes and drive to work on Monday morning from that area. You’ll find out very quickly whether or not you want to do this on a permanent basis.
