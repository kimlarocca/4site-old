Home Inspections Just Don't Benefit Buyers
 

Only one chance to make one, they say. The truth is that this axiom applies to agents as well as buyers. If you can assist your listing agent in getting other agents excited about your property, you have a very good chance of selling quickly and at a good price.

If you can, go to open houses in your area and see what you think about these properties. Think like a buyer. Determine what looks good and what doesn’t?

Your home doesn’t have to qualify for the Architectural Digest to make a good impression, but there are some tips that can accentuate the positives and downplay the negatives.

  • Get rid of excess stuff. We all have it and you probably don’t want to move it - again. Do yard sales, donate it, or put it on E-Bay. You’re selling your house, not all your things. NO CLUTTER!
  • If paint or fix-ups are needed, do it.
  • Keep countertops clear. The buyers want to see how big that kitchen island is, not how many appliances you have.
  • Keep the bathrooms and kitchen clean. It’s hard to do, but buyers are on their own schedule and won’t wait for you to clean.
  • Landscaping - at least have the front yard in order. Many buyers dismiss homes without getting out of the car because of the front yard. Don’t let this happen to you.
  • Exterior- does it look well kept?
  • NO CLUTTER!- Yes it’s repeated, but it is that important.
