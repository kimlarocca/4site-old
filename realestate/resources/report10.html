Home Inspections Just Don't Benefit Buyers
 

Just as in Economics 101, the temperature of the market is determined by supply and demand. There are other factors, such as interest rates, but ultimately they all lead back to the basics - how many sellers and how many buyers there are.

Lots of sellers and few buyers = a buyer’s market with soft prices. As a buyer this is a market where you can get very good deals. A motivated seller will be willing to bargain in order to move their house quickly. If you are a seller in this sort of market, your home needs to stand out in some way - unique features, unique location or unique pricing. High interest rates can also contribute to this sort of market. In that case the seller might be willing to assist in some manner with the financing.

Lots of buyers and few sellers = a seller’s market with multiple offers on homes. Sales for more than the listing price is not uncommon. If you are a buyer in this sort of market, you absolutely need to be ready to move and quickly. You need your financing in place and ready to go. There’s no time for waiting around here.

The motivations of both the buyer and the seller always are always a factor to consider. No matter the market type, a seller who needs to sell and a buyer who needs to buy can change everything. That’s where a good agent comes in. They can get you the best deal available no matter what the temperature of the market.
